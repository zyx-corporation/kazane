# Design Principles

Kazane design principles are not slogans. They are decision rules for trade-offs.

## 1. Context sovereignty over automation

If automation causes the organization to lose control of context, Kazane should slow down, stop, or require human review.

## 2. Responsible escalation over blind completion

An AI agent stopping with a clear reason is a successful outcome when responsibility boundaries are reached.

## 3. Written handoff over ephemeral chat

Important work should move through durable notes, work items, evidence, and audit trails rather than untraceable conversation.

## 4. Evidence-linked summaries over detached summaries

Summaries are useful only when users can trace them back to source evidence.

## 5. Structured data over UI-only state

Humans need usable UI. AI agents need stable, structured, machine-readable state. Kazane must support both.

## 6. Local-first over cloud convenience for sensitive context

Cloud deployment is allowed, but should not be the default assumption for sensitive organizational context.

## 7. Small-organization depth over generic enterprise breadth

Kazane should serve the deep needs of small teams, founders, operators, and AI番頭-style workflows before trying to cover every enterprise workflow.

## 8. Review chains over single-agent authority

Important outputs should pass through role-specific review, RDE audit, or human approval.

## 9. Operations health over agent self-report

AI saying completed is not sufficient. The system must detect missing evidence, stale work, failed writes, broken links, and silent failure.

## 10. Reversible decisions where possible

Kazane should preserve enough history to revisit, revise, and reverse decisions whenever possible.
